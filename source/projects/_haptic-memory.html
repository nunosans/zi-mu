Haptic Memory

Masters of Art (MA) in Graphic Design Final Project
2013

“How can books act as a haptic media to engage viewers in an interactive experience of the senses in this digital age?’”

It uses the simplest approaches to interpret the importance and role of haptic feeling in the book. My book uses the concept of ‘no ink’ to simply represent the ways in which the physical book can offer an interactive experience through the sense of touch. My project explored the use of printed books as a medium to connect and communicate with audiences. I used the tactile, physical characteristics of printed books to demonstrate the ways in which book design can be an important medium to transmit information.

My final work aims to stimulate people to rethink the value of the printed book in this digital age. I grouped tactile memories into two categories, and then I used images and texts to tell the story behind each pattern.
